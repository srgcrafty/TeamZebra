Habitat Information

Plains zebra are found on the savannas from Sudan to northern Zimbabwe in eastern Africa. Grevy's zebras are now mostly restricted to parts of northern Kenya. Mountain zebras occur in southwestern Africa with cape mountain zebras in South Africa and Hartmann's mountain zebras in Namibia and Angola.

Mating



Gestation: 12-13 Months

Zebras generally have just one offspring at a time.

Offspring live with their parents for 11-16 months before living independantly.

Behavior of Zebras

Zebras sleep standing up, and only when they are in a herd that can warn them of danger.

If they spot a predator, they will bark or whine loudly to warn the others.

They feed by grazing in large groups.

Their herds are called Harems. They are very social animals.
